Study Module

1. What is Motivation?

Motivation is the internal drive or desire to achieve a goal in language learning. It plays a key role in second language acquisition (SLA), influencing effort, persistence, and overall success.

According to Dörnyei (2015), motivation is the prerequisite for all other factors in SLA.

2. Why is Motivation Crucial?

  • Compensates for lack of aptitude or poor conditions
  • Keeps learners engaged during challenges
  • Drives learners toward goals despite difficulties (Dörnyei, 2015)
  • Closely tied to attitude (Gardner & Lambert, 1972)

3. Types of Motivation

  • Intrinsic: Personal interest, curiosity, enjoyment (e.g., learning to understand a culture)
  • Extrinsic: Driven by rewards, pressure (e.g., grades, promotions)

4. Learning Strategies

Learning strategies are specific actions learners take to improve acquisition, such as:

  • Memorization, practice routines
  • Monitoring, planning, and summarizing
  • Functional vs. formal use (Bialystok, 1981)

5. Oxford’s Strategy Categories

  • Memory
  • Cognitive
  • Compensation
  • Metacognitive
  • Affective
  • Social

These strategies help learners plan, manage emotions, and interact effectively.

6. Learning Styles

Styles reflect how individuals prefer to process information. Common types include:

  • Visual, auditory, kinesthetic, reading/writing
  • Planner vs. spontaneous, independent vs. group

7. Individual Differences (IDs)

  • Cognitive: Aptitude, memory
  • Affective: Motivation, attitude
  • Environmental: Teaching methods, exposure

Understanding IDs helps teachers personalize learning and support diverse learners.

8. SLA Principles

  • L2 learning is harder than L1
  • Most learners don’t reach native-like fluency
  • Affective factors are crucial
  • Input alone isn’t enough – interaction is key
  • No one perfect method of instruction

9. Assessment in SLA

Effective assessment helps guide instruction and motivate learners. Key ideas:

  • Use both formative and summative methods
  • Provide feedback to support progress
  • Encourage second chances and correction

10. Alternative Assessment

  • Focus on real-world performance
  • Includes projects, self-assessment, and peer feedback
  • Gives ongoing feedback instead of one-time grades

This helps learners understand their strengths and areas for improvement.
